# Understanding the Honor Flight Guardian Database
> (Generated by AI based on model and schema details in this codebase.)

The Honor Flight program relies on dedicated guardians to accompany veterans on their trip to Washington, D.C. This overview explains how guardian information is organized and used to ensure each guardian is prepared, qualified, and matched with the right veteran for a safe and meaningful mission.

## The Guardian's Journey
When someone applies to serve as a guardian, we create a detailed digital profile that follows them from application through training and flight day. Starting with basic information like name and contact details, the profile grows to include everything needed to confirm their readiness and coordinate their role on the trip.

## Personal Background
Each guardian profile begins with personal details needed for communication and logistics. Beyond name and birth date, we record contact information, occupation, and whether the guardian is themselves a veteran. An emergency contact is always on file so staff can reach someone quickly if needed during the trip.

## Training and Readiness
Guardians must complete training before they fly, and the system tracks every step of that process. We record the type of training completed—whether in-person, by phone, online, or as a make-up session—and whether training is fully complete. Payment status is tracked, including fee exemptions where applicable. Guardians also receive program materials, and we record how many books have been ordered. Notes from training sessions, including any requirement to see a doctor before flying, help staff follow up until every guardian is cleared to serve.

## Health and Physical Capability
Because guardians provide hands-on support to veterans, we maintain detailed medical and physical capability information. The system tracks medical forms and releases, dietary restrictions, and any medical limitations. Guardians are rated on a simple A–D scale for medical level, and we record whether they can push a wheelchair or lift up to 100 pounds. This information is essential for matching guardians with veterans whose needs they can safely meet.

## Pairing with Veterans
The heart of a guardian's role is their relationship with the veteran they serve. The system maintains current pairings with veteran names and IDs, along with preference notes that guide matching decisions. Every pairing and unpairing event is recorded in history, creating a clear record of who was assigned to whom and when changes were made.

## Flight Planning and Coordination
As guardians move toward their flight date, the system coordinates all aspects of their trip assignment. Each guardian is assigned to a specific flight and bus group (designated as Alpha or Bravo teams), complete with seat assignments. We track confirmation dates, flight status, and required paperwork—including liability waivers, media waivers, and health-related documentation. Shirt sizes and other apparel details ensure guardians receive properly fitting Honor Flight gear.

## Keeping Everyone Connected
Communication is key to preparing guardians for their role. The database tracks outreach efforts, including which staff member is assigned to call each guardian, whether email or mail has been sent, and notes from those conversations. We also record how each guardian heard about the program, which helps us understand and improve our recruitment efforts. For the Mail Call program, we maintain contact information for family members who wish to send letters and cards to their guardian during the trip.

## Apparel and Logistics
Beyond basic shirt sizing, the system tracks jacket and polo orders, delivery method, and when items were sent. Staff can see at a glance whether a guardian's gear has been mailed, delivered at training, or sent to their home.

## Living History
Perhaps most importantly, the system maintains a detailed history of each guardian's journey with Honor Flight. Every significant change or update is recorded with a timestamp and the name of the staff member who made it—covering flight assignments, training milestones, payment, medical paperwork, and veteran pairings. This creates a living document that helps us provide the best possible experience while maintaining the trust placed in us by our guardians and the veterans they serve.

This comprehensive approach ensures that from the moment a guardian applies until they return from their Honor Flight, we have the information needed to prepare them for one of the most meaningful roles in the program. The system helps us transform complex logistics into seamless, safe experiences for the volunteers who make each Honor Flight mission possible.
