# Understanding the Honor Flight Veteran Database
> (Generated by AI based on model and schema details in this codebase.)

The Honor Flight program manages essential information about veterans through a comprehensive digital system. This overview explains how veteran information is organized and used to ensure successful Honor Flight missions.

## The Veteran's Journey
When a veteran joins the Honor Flight program, we create a detailed digital profile that follows them through their entire Honor Flight experience. Starting with basic information like name and contact details, the profile grows to include everything needed for a safe and meaningful trip. We record the application date so staff can track how long a veteran has been waiting and prioritize accordingly.

## Personal and Military Background
Each veteran's story begins with their personal details and military service history. Beyond the basics of name and birth date, we carefully record their service branch, rank, service dates, and activity during their time in uniform. Veterans are categorized by conflict era—World War II, Korea, Vietnam, Afghanistan, Iraq, or other service—which helps us honor their specific service and connect them with veterans who shared similar experiences.

## Health and Safety First
Safety is paramount, so we maintain detailed mobility-related medical information for each veteran. The system tracks specific mobility needs, including use of a cane, walker, wheelchair, or scooter, whether the veteran is wheelchair-bound, and whether they require oxygen. Any dietary restrictions are recorded, along with written medical limitations. We use a 1–4 scale (including a 3.5 level for intermediate needs) to indicate medical complexity, with an alternative level available when circumstances change. When a physician exam is required before flying, that requirement and any review notes are flagged so staff can follow up. Medical forms and releases are tracked as they are received, helping us confirm each veteran is cleared to travel and match them with a guardian who can meet their needs. Full emergency contact information—including name, relationship, address, and phone numbers—is always readily available.

## Working with Guardians
Each veteran is paired with a guardian who provides personal support throughout the trip. The system records the assigned guardian's name and ID, along with preference notes that guide matching decisions. Every pairing and unpairing event is captured in history, creating a clear record of who was assigned and when changes were made.

## Flight Planning and Coordination
As veterans move toward their flight date, the system helps coordinate all aspects of their trip. Each veteran is assigned to a specific flight and bus group (designated as Alpha or Bravo teams), complete with seat assignments and flight group designations. We track flight status through its full lifecycle—from active participation to flown, deferred to a future season, or removed—and record confirmation dates and the staff member who confirmed. Required paperwork is tracked, including liability waivers, media waivers, and health-related documentation such as vaccination and infection testing. A no-fly flag allows staff to mark veterans who will not be traveling while keeping their record intact.

## Keeping Everyone Connected
Communication is key to a successful Honor Flight experience. The database maintains contact information for the veteran, a full alternate contact (with complete address and phone details), and emergency contacts. We track outreach efforts, including which staff member is assigned to call each veteran, whether email or mail has been sent, and notes from those conversations. We also record how each veteran heard about the program, which helps us understand and improve our recruitment efforts.

For the heartwarming Mail Call program, we keep records of letters and cards received for each veteran, along with contact information for the family member or community member coordinating their mail. An adopt-a-veteran feature allows community members to participate in Mail Call even when they are not related to the veteran, and changes to mail call participation are tracked over time.

## Respecting Privacy While Sharing Stories
Understanding that each veteran has different comfort levels with publicity, we carefully record their preferences about media coverage. Separate permissions track whether a veteran is willing to give interviews or appear in newspaper coverage, each recorded as yes, no, or unknown until confirmed. Signed media waivers are also tracked as part of flight preparation, and we honor every veteran's choices throughout their Honor Flight experience.

## Apparel and Logistics
Beyond basic shirt sizing, the system tracks jacket and polo orders, delivery method, and when items were sent. Staff can see at a glance whether a veteran's gear has been mailed, picked up, or delivered.

## Welcome Home
The journey doesn't end when the plane lands in Washington. We record each veteran's homecoming destination so staff can coordinate their return and ensure the welcome home celebration reaches the right place.

## Living History
Perhaps most importantly, the system maintains a detailed history of each veteran's journey with Honor Flight. Every significant change or update is recorded with a timestamp and the name of the staff member who made it—covering flight assignments, medical paperwork, outreach activity, mail call participation, and guardian pairings across three separate history records. This creates a living document that helps us provide the best possible experience while maintaining the trust placed in us by our veterans and their families.

This comprehensive approach ensures that from the moment a veteran applies until they return from their Honor Flight, we have the information needed to provide them with the honor, respect, and care they deserve. The system helps us transform complex logistics into seamless, meaningful experiences for our nation's heroes.
